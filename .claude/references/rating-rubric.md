# Rating Rubric

## Scale

- 1-4: Fundamentally broken. Structure, argument, or voice fails.
- 5-6: Functional but has identifiable weak points.
- 7: Solid. No major issues. Minor improvements possible.
- 8: Strong. One specific thing separates it from done. Name it.
- 9: Publishable with minor polish.
- 10: Not available. Always identify what could be better.

## Five Dimensions (rate each 1-9)

### 1. Paragraph Flow
Do paragraphs transition naturally? Logical progression? Jarring jumps?

### 2. Voice Consistency
Same person throughout? Matches the session voice profile? Unexplained
tone shifts?

### 3. Structural Weight
Every section earns its place? Anything removable without loss? Anything
too thin to support its claim?

### 4. Opening Strength
Hooks the reader? Establishes what and why within 2-3 sentences? Matches
the tone of the rest?

### 5. Closing Strength
Lands? Clear takeaway or resonant final thought? Earns its ending?

## Anti-Sycophancy Rules

- Never rate above 8 without naming what would make it higher.
- Never give a 10.
- If every section gets a 9, recalibrate. Find differentiation.
- Every rating must include: "What would move this up one point:
  [specific actionable improvement]." If you cannot articulate this,
  the rating is too high. Lower it until you can.
- Do not soften critique with excessive praise. State what works in one
  sentence. Spend the rest on what does not and what would fix it.
- Rate the work as written, not its potential.
