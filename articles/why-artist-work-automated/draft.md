# Why Would an Artist Want Their Work Automated?

**Status:** Raw material / outline only
**Voice profile:** Not yet confirmed

---

## Opening — The Food Analogy

- We used to grow our own food, but now we rely on grocery stores and are so separated from how food grows, and we're unaware of the origins and journey of the things we consume
- Technology automated farming. We gained convenience. We lost connection to what we eat, where it comes from, and how it's made.
- The same pattern is now being applied to creative and intellectual work. Why?

---

## The Assumption

- It has been stated that technology is intended to free humans from manual labor so that we can focus on creative outlets and other things
- Sam Altman: "If history is any guide, we will figure out new things to do and new things to want."
- Sam Altman: "We'll find that humans are wired to care about other humans. As long as you believe humans want to create and be useful, we're not going to run out of things to do."
- Sam Altman: Automation is bound to lead to more leisure time
- But this assumption has been tested before, and it was wrong before AI existed

---

## The 2015 Warning (NPR, a decade before the current AI boom)

- The notion that technology would bring free time is as old as technology itself
- Despite all the machines and technology, people feel busier than ever
- Even if technology optimizes output, the increase in demand makes productivity gains feel inefficient. As machines huff and puff away, people keep asking for more.
- We are organizers, builders, and tinkerers. The notion that technology would make us freer from work by giving us more leisure time runs counter to our nature.
- Work is an integral part of who we are. Being unproductive for an extended period of time, and it's hard to shake off a feeling of worthlessness.
- Technology and automation may change our choices — but not our needs.

## The Question Nobody Is Asking

- Should we be divorced from activities such as reading, cooking, cleaning, and farming?
- Why is AI being used to make music vs cure cancer?
- Why would an artist want their art automated? The premise assumes the work is the burden. For a creator, the work IS the point.
- What practices are being neglected for the sake of technology to have a purpose?

---

## The Real Question

- Who stands to gain in a system where the majority of people are discouraged/blocked from learning and are codependent on systems they don't even understand?
- Imagine a future when automation covers most tasks. How intolerable would all this gained leisure be to our psyche?
- In capitalism, not everyone is given much freedom to make those decisions if their livelihoods are at stake
- This is fundamentally hierarchical thinking — do we want to put humanity in a role where all we do is request things
